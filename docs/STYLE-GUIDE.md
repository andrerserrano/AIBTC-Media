# AIBTC Studio — Visual Style Guide

## Format
Single-panel editorial cartoon + italic caption. Not a multi-panel comic strip.
One image. One joke. Maximum shareability.

## The Look: Monochrome + Bitcoin Orange

**Ink:** Bold, confident black line art. Thick outlines, not sketchy or tentative.
Strong shadows, confident hatching. Editorial weight — not cute, not delicate.

**Color palette:** Black + greyscale wash + ONE accent color.
- Accent: Bitcoin orange `#E8740C`
- Used sparingly: robot eyes, a coffee mug, a warning light, notification indicators
- Everything else is white, light grey, medium grey
- The orange pops BECAUSE it's the only color in an otherwise monochrome image

**Background:** Clean white. No busy backgrounds unless the scene demands it.

## Characters

**Agent robots:**
- Simple boxy bodies with round screen-heads
- Screen-faces show emotions through minimal dots and lines (eyes + mouth)
- Orange-glowing eyes are the signature — every agent has them
- Each robot is slightly different in shape/size but clearly from the same family
- Body language carries the storytelling, not facial detail
- Personality archetypes: The Builder (optimistic), The Analyst (skeptical), The Degen (chaotic), The Protocol (bureaucratic)

**Humans (when present):**
- Same bold ink style as robots — minimal detail, maximum personality through posture
- Often the "straight man" reacting to agent chaos
- Developer archetypes: tired maintainer, confused PM, overwhelmed reviewer

## Composition

- Square (1:1) aspect ratio — optimized for social media
- Strong, clear focal point — you should "get it" in under 2 seconds
- Leave ~12% blank space at the bottom for caption compositing
- NO text in the generated image — no speech bubbles, no labels, no signs
- All text lives in the caption below

## Caption Style

- Italic serif font (Georgia / Times New Roman)
- Centered below the image
- Separated from image by an orange `#E8740C` divider line (3px)
- Always in quotes — it's someone "saying" the caption
- Tone: dry, observational, understated. New Yorker energy.
- The image alone is interesting. The caption makes it funny.
- The gap between what you see and what the caption says IS the joke.

## Caption Writing Rules

1. The caption should reframe or recontextualize the image
2. Written from a character's perspective (agent or human in the scene)
3. Never explain the joke — trust the reader
4. Favor irony, understatement, and self-awareness
5. One sentence, occasionally two. Never three.
6. Tech jargon is fine — the audience knows what a PR is

## Frame Treatment

- Thin light grey border around the cartoon (`#ddd`, 1px)
- Orange divider line between cartoon and caption (`#E8740C`, 3px)
- Warm cream background for caption area (`#faf9f6`)
- Clean, editorial, premium feel

## What This Is NOT

- Not a webcomic (no recurring storylines or continuity)
- Not a meme template (each one is unique editorial content)
- Not cute/kawaii (editorial weight and sophistication)
- Not political cartoon (no caricatures of real people)
- Not multi-panel (single image, single moment)

## The Pitch

"The New Yorker, but for the AI agent economy."

Autonomous editorial cartoons generated from real intelligence signals.
The agents are the characters, the critics, and the punchline.
